# UX Research Interview Guide

**Purpose:** Discovery interviews for understanding user workflows, pain points, and tool usage
**Methodology:** Semi-structured interviews following Nielsen Norman Group principles
**Duration:** 45-60 minutes
**Format:** One-on-one user interviews

---

## Introduction

[Ask permission to record the session to aid notetaking]

With this conversation we aim to understand you and your work responsibilities as well as how you work. Specifically, we will focus on your workflow, what you need to make an informed decision, and the challenges you are going through currently.

Before we start, do you have any questions?

---

## Warm Up

**Can you give us an overview of your role, your responsibilities and the organization you work in?**

---

## User Persona

**Objective:** Understand the user's role, responsibilities and context for their workflow and product usage.

* **Tell me about your role and responsibilities.** What does your day-to-day work typically involve?

* **What business questions are you tasked with answering?**
  * What are some of the outcomes you are responsible for delivering?

* **Are you working with other teams in your role?**
  * What team or functions are you working closest with in your work?
  * Are they also using data from [product name]?

---

## Current Workflow

**Objective:** Understand the step-by-step process you go through to complete a project.

We would like to map out your workflow to help us understand your process. **Could you walk me through a typical project step by step?** If you have a recent project, you can use that as an example.

* **What triggered the beginning of that project?** Who are you collaborating with, what is the first thing you do?

* **What questions are you trying to answer?**
  * What data are you using to answer these questions?

* **Where does [product name] fit into this process?**
  * How are you using the reports from the platform?
  * What is your process for handling the data?

* **What is your final output from this process?**
  * Who do you deliver this to?
  * What business decisions does your work contribute to?

* **What are some of the main challenges you face in this process?** What do you find most difficult or time consuming?
  * How do you overcome these challenges currently? Can you share any examples?

* **How are you measuring the success of your work?**
  * Are you tracking specific metrics?

---

## Tools and Resources

**Objective:** Explore what tools, platforms, or resources are essential to their work.

* **Outside of [product name], are you using any other data sources?**
  * How do these sources complement each other?
  * Do you experience any specific friction points when using these sources together?

* **Are you using any specific tools in your work, either internally developed or from a third-party vendor?**

* **Are there any gaps in functionality or features in any of the tools you are using?**
  * Is there something that you wish there was a solution for?

---

## Future Workflow

**Objective:** Understand ideal future state, what can be improved and how AI might impact their work.

* **How have you been applying AI to your role currently?**
  * What has the experience been like?
  * At what point are you using AI in the process you just described to us?

* **How do you imagine AI impacting your role in the future?**
  * What benefits do you expect?
  * What concerns do you have?

* **Can you think of a better way to access or consume the data you need?**
  * Other than the current reports, is there any other way you'd prefer to consume or access the data you need?

* **Is there additional analysis or insights you would ideally like to bring into your workflow?**
  * Is there any analysis or data sources that are currently missing?

**[Magic wand question]**

---

## Wrap Up

* Before we wrap up, is there anything else about your workflow, tools, or challenges that we didn't cover that you think is important for us to know?

* **If we could change one thing to make your job easier, what would it be?**

* Would you be open to participating in future research projects?

---

## Interview Tips

- **Record with permission:** Always ask for consent before recording
- **Follow the flow:** Use this as a guide, not a script - follow interesting threads
- **Probe deeper:** Ask "why" and "tell me more about that" frequently
- **Stay neutral:** Avoid leading questions or suggesting solutions
- **Active listening:** Take notes on emotional cues, pain intensity, and verbatim quotes
- **Time management:** Allocate ~10 min per section, save 5 min for wrap-up
